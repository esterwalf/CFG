Spring Birds!

When the weather warms up, we come out

A Swallow: The First Sign of Spring
Conservation status
Green

How to identify us

We're pretty flighty and spend most of our time in the air. But if you happen to spot us, we are small birds with dark, glossy-blue backs, red throats, a pale underbody and long tail streamers.

Where to find us

We are breeding in strong numbers, so our conservation status is green, but regional numbers may vary significantly! We like to inhabit urban and suburban areas, farmland, wetland and grassland.

Osprey Bird
Conservation Status
Not Assessed

How to identify us

If you don't look carefully, you might mistake me for a gull! Our underparts are white or slightly mottled, our wings are angled and bend at the wrist, and our wings have contrasting white linings.

Where to find us

Unfortunately, our numbers have been in decline due to illegal killings and low breeding numbers, but you can find us in marine and wetland areas.

Black Cap Bird
Conservation Status
Green

How to identify us

The top of our males' head is black (hence the name) and the top of our females' head is chestnut. Our body is a greyish colour and we're pretty small!

Where to find us

We are mainly UK visitors, coming over from Germany and north-East Europe as the weather warms up. But more and more, we're spending our winters in the UK and you can spot us in woodland, urban and suburban areas, and farmland.

Yellow Wagtail Bird
Conservation Status
Red

How to identify us

We are small, yellow and green, with a medium-length tail and slender black legs. We spend most of our time walking or running on the ground.

Where to find us

We arrive in Spring and spend Summer in the UK, before migrating to Africa for the winter. We breed in a number of UK habitats, including farmland, wet pastures and meadows, but unfortunately, our breeding numbers have been in serious decline.
