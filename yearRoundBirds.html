Year-Round Birds

These birds are seen throughout the year

House Sparrow
Conservation status
Red

Key characteristics

Sparrows are small birds with feathers ranging from pale cream to dark brown. They have sharp, pointed beaks which enable them to eat small insects.

Where to find them

These characterful birds can be found all around the world; however, moniotoring suggests that their population has declined by 71% in the last 40 years.

Blue Tit
Conservation Status
Green

Key characteristics

The Blue Tit is one of the most easily recogisable garden birds, thanks to its colourful mix of blue, yellow, white and green feathers and small, pointed beak.

Where to find them

Blue Tits can be found all over the country, but they are particularly prevalent in woodland or suburbn areas.

Siskin
Conservation Status
Green

Key characteristics

The Siskin is a small, brightly coloured bird with a distinctive, vibrant green plumage, forked tail and narrow beak.

Where to find them

Siskins can be found all acoss the UK, but are most commonly found in Scotland and Wales.

Woodpigeon
Conservation Status
Amber

Key characteristics

The Woodpigeon is the largest and most common pigeon in the UK. It is mosly grey with a white neck patch and white wing patches. Its most distinctive feature is its cooing call.

Where to find them

Woodpigeons can be found in almost any area of the UK, but are more likely to be seen in suburban town areas.
