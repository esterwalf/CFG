Autumn Birds!

These birds emerge as the leaves turn red and the air grows crisp

A Tawny Owl: In autumn, Tawny Owls become more vocal as they establish territories and prepare for the upcoming breeding season.
Conservation Status: Amber

Key characteristics;

  • Tawny Owls are 37-39cm in lenght and weigh 350-590g.
  • They have a wingspan of 94-104cm!
  • They possess remarkable hearing abilities, allowing them to easily locate their prey even in complete darkness!

    • Where to find them: Tawny Owls can be found in woodlands, urban and suburban areas and farmlands.

    Redwing
    Conservation Status: Not Assessed.

    Key characteristics: Redwings are 21cm in lenght, and weigh 50-75g.

    Where to find them: Redwings can be found in woodlands, urban and suburban areas, farmland and grassland.

    Pink-footed Goose
    Conservation Status: Not Assessed.

    Key characteristics: They have pink feet! Their feathers are brown, grey and white.

    Where to find them: These geese can be found in Marine and Intertidal areas, farmland, wetland and grassland.

    Fieldfare
    Conservation Status: Red

    Key Characteristics: They have a distinctive gray head, gray back, and chestnut-brown wings and tail. Fieldfares are around 23-27 cm in length.

    Where to find them: Fieldfares can be found in woodland, urban and suburban areas, farmland and grassland.
